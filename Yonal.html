Scientists Utilise Deep Learning With Satellites To Count Elephants From Outer Space!

By Yonal Ariyananda

People love to talk about the phenomenon that is space.

Not just space, but what you can see from it.

We all know that we can see massive landmarks from space, such as the pyramids or the Great wall of China.

But what if we could see far smaller objects or beings? Something like Elephants.

Luckily for us, a team of researchers may have found way for this to happen, and consequently ensuring the welfare and survival of many wildlife species going forwards.

Who are these researchers?

The researchers responsible for this breakthrough comes from the Wildlife Conservation Research Unit, University of Oxford,

And the Machine Learning Department, University of Bath.
