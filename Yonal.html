Scientists Utilise Deep Learning With Satellites To Count Elephants From Outer Space!

By Yonal Ariyananda

People love to talk about the phenomenon that is space.

Not just space, but what you can see from it.

We all know that we can see massive landmarks from space, such as the pyramids or the Great wall of China.

But what if we could see far smaller objects or beings? Something like Elephants.

Luckily for us, a team of researchers may have found way for this to happen, and consequently ensuring the welfare and survival of many wildlife species going forwards.

Why is it important to count elephants?

The population of African elpehants has plummeted over the last century due to various reasons such as poaching,retaliatory killing for crop raiding and habitat fragmentation.

Protection of these elephants depends heavily on know exactly where they are and how many exist- accurate monitering is extremely vital.

The existing methods however, are prone to error.

And inaccurate counts leads to misallocation of scarce conservation resources and misunderstanding population trends.

Who are these researchers?

The researchers responsible for this breakthrough comes from the Wildlife Conservation Research Unit, University of Oxford,

And the Machine Learning Department, University of Bath.

What have they done that's so impressive?

For the first time ever, scientists have been able to utilise satellite cameras coupled with deep learning to count animals in complex geographical landscapes.

They have done so by using imagery from Maxar satellites, which opens a whole new way to moniter vulnerable and endangered animals.

How have we been surveying elephant populations uptil now?

Right now the most common survey technique for elephant populations in savannah environments are aerial counts from manned aircraft.

However, this presents a myriad of problems such as follows:

  • Observers on aerial surveys can get exhausted, be hindered by poor visibility and otherwise succumb to bias.
  • Aerial surveys can also be costly and logistically challenging.

    • Advantages of this method when compared to human detection.

  • Eliminates the risk of Double counting.
  • Allows for repeat surveys at short intervals.
  • Requires no ground prescence hence no animals are disturbed.
  • Previously inaccesible areas become accessible
  • Cross-border areas can be surveyed without the time-consuming need of obtaining terrestrial permits.
  • The Deep learning system is able to process enormous amounts of data in a matter of hours(humans would have taken months).
  • The results from Deep Learning are also more consistent, less prone to error, false negatives and false positives.
  • This system can also be rectifies by continously improving models- which cannot be said for humans.
